About the Project

We built this site to demonstrate that ranking systems can be built with the focus of educating graduate students and the public instead of opinionating them. The CS Rankings project is a good step in the direction of building fairer and more transparent systems for ranking. We have tried to build an extension of it that communicates a different view of the rankings.

The By School and By Area tabs

We have identified that there are broadly two ways of looking at CS school rankings: a) looking at a particular school's overall research portfolio and b) looking at how different schools compete against each other in a particular area/combination of areas. This is the reason we have provided two views here. The By School view might be useful for graduate students to understand departments they would like to apply to or have offers from. The By Area view is the more conventional view of rankings.

Not all areas are the same

Even when looking at rankings in a particular area, it is important to understand that not all rankings look the same. Very often, students have preconceived notions of prestige which might not hold true in all areas. To avoid this pitfall, we clearly show the Spearman's rank correlation with the "All Areas" rank of whatever areas are selected in the By Area view. Our main motive is to communicate that if an area has low correlation with the default ranking then students need to take a deeper look at the research and set aside their notions of institutional prestige. All rankings here are computed by aggregating research from year 2000 onwards.

Statistics over a distribution of rankings

Moreover, we have observed that given the combinatorial flexibility of the CS Rankings system, there are 2areas ways to build a ranking and we have a distribution over ranks for each department. If somehow, certain attributes of this distribution could be communicated with the students, it might be beneficial to them. To this end, we compute stats like the mode, median, first and third quartile ranks out of each school's distributions. These stats are shown in the Ranking Statistics panel in the By School view. We compute these stats on a random sample of 200,000 rankings out of the total 2areas rankings. We have also tried to highlight here any unconventional strenghts that a school might have; if an area's rank is 1.5 standard deviations above the computed median, it is mentioned as an unconventional strength for that school.

Faculty listing by area

Eventually, we realize that rankings are only a small part of the complex problem of graduate school applications and that not all information can be computed. So we list faculty members that have published in the top venues in an area in the past 5 years and list down their homepages in the Faculty Listing section of the By School view. This is merely to enable the students to explore the faculty's research on their own, and make their own decisions thereon.
